FIRST PROTECT YOURSELF!
Due to Homeowner Insurance policy exclusions, many of us are left to clean up water-damaged homes and contents without the benefit of insurance claim monies to pay a professional Remediation Company. It is this type of situation that can lead to future indoor air quality problems from continuing microbial ( bacterial and fungal ) growths inside our homes.

Using a professional water Restoration Company is always the best and safest choice. However, if that is not financially feasible........ below are some guidelines to follow, that will assist you in minimizing the chances of future problems from persisting bacteria or mold growths.

Most important: Protect yourself, your family and pets from contact with bacteria-laden water. During clean up, wear safety goggles, latex gloves and a respirator or N-95 particle mask. Avoid contact with the water and bacteria on your skin and eyes, ears and nasal passages. Don't sleep or stay in water damaged rooms. The elderly and young children are the most susceptible to develop illnesses from exposure to infectious bacteria and molds.
  1. Get rid of the standing water as quick as possible. Don't leave wet areas to dry out over time. Use wet-dry vacs, sub-pumps or squeegees to remove the muddy, contaminated water.
  2. Wash all hard floor surfaces such as concrete, vinyl tile, sheet vinyl and ceramic tile with a bleach water solution and TSP powder cleaner, ( ½ gal bleach, ½ gal water and 4 oz. of TSP powder cleaner) Lysol concentrated anti-microbial cleaners are also effective and produce a pleasant smell.
  3. Ventilate, Ventilate, Ventilate. Open windows and doors on a sunny clear day and bring fresh air in and out of your home to dry out and remove odors and VOC ( volatile organic compounds ) from cleaning solutions and contaminated water.
  4. Use whatever fans you have to move the air through the wet areas. If your Air conditioning system was not in the water, use the fan setting to move air through continuously through the duct system and home to aide in evaporation. Use dehumidifiers to dry out areas that can't be reached by ventilation or high air movement.
  5. Don't try to save wall-to-wall carpeting after it has been wet for more than 72 hours. The level of bacteria and mold after this time is too far developed to able to remove them from the porous materials. Wear safety protection and remove both carpet and pad and dispose of it. Sanitize the exposed floor surface and dry it out as soon as possible.
  6. Hardwood floors should be dried out from below the sub-floor. Direct drying fans and dehumidifiers blowing from below the floor as possible.
  7. Drywall and paper-backed insulation that has remained wet for more than 72 hours should also be removed and the wood 2" x 4"s wiped down with a Lysol solution and dried out prior to replacing the drywall and any paper-backed fiberglass insulation.
  8. Open closets, storage areas and crawl spaces and dry them out and also using the same guidelines.

NOTE: The drying out process of the floors, walls and structure is the most critical step and may take many days or even weeks to accomplish. Time is of the essence! Don't start the renovations until all the areas are dried out .

Contents Exposed To Ground Water:
  1. If anything is dish washer safe, put it in and run the hottest and longest cleaning cycle.
  2. Plastic toys, metals, glass and synthetic materials can be wiped down with Lysol or 70% Isopropyl alcohol. Just remember, alcohol is very flammable and caution must be used not to use near a flame of any kind.
  3. Wood furniture needs to be dried out quickly. Put outside in the sun and wipe exposed areas with a slightly damp cloth containing Murphy's oil soap. If drawers were wet, wipe out the unfinished surfaces with Lysol and put them out in the sun also. Waxes, polyurethane or tung oil finishing can be applied after these steps when they are completely dry.
  4. Washable synthetic rugs need to be machine washed and dried out in the sun if possible. If they are too large to wash that way, get them dried out ASAP, treat them with Lysol prior to drying them out. An additional cleaning step will be needed after the are dried out. Spray with Lysol after the cleaning also. .
  5. Upholstered furniture needs to be dried out completely. Wipe down the wet fabric areas with alcohol while drying, ( color-fast test first ) Lysol can also be used.
  6. Don't try to save wet mattresses or box springs. These are too porous to effectively dry and sanitize. Replace them. HVAC Systems.
  7. If your unit or ductwork was exposed to the water, it needs to be professionally cleaned and sanitized prior to re-use and the filter changed.

NOTE: If after these steps are completed, odor persists or mold growth appears, additional investigation is needed prior to starting renovations. Conducting indoor air quality testing after the drying and clean up stages are completed is advisable to be sure there is not an elevated level of airborne bacteria or fungal spores in the indoor air.
