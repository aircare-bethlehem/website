Subject: From a highly satisfied and appreciative customer...

Air Care and Restoration of Bethlehem, Pennsylvania provides a complete set of services for homeowners to address, in a thoughtful and effective manner, the variety of problems associated with water infiltration and mold damage. Their solutions are well-conceived and long-lasting, and follow-up visits validate their responsiveness to the homeowner.

When I purchased my 24 year-old house in 2001, I thought that the mildew-type odors throughout the house could be corrected with an air filter, especially since my home inspection did not reveal any systemic problem. Air Care, using thorough and advanced diagnostic techniques, discovered that the problem was more invasive and could not be corrected simply with an air filter.

With state-of-the-art technology, they identified that a number of walls had higher than normal moisture content. Further investigation located a cracked foundation, which Air Care repaired. To stem the flow of water around the perimeter of the house, Air Care completely regraded the landscape. Other contractors had proposed expensive French drains that become inoperative after a few years.

The Air Care crew also determined that the house was getting insufficient light because of the expanse of foliage that surrounded the property. This prevented the siding and roof from properly drying after a rainy or humid day. Air Care then proceeded to contract with a tree removal service to tactically cut back the offending trees, while leaving the bulk of the remaining trees intact. Also, extra drainage pipes were installed along the roofline and along the regraded landscaping. Special vents were added to certain walls to moderate the humidity levels.

To address the abnormally high humidity throughout the interior of the house, Air Care installed an air management system of which no other contractor I had surveyed was aware. This system has certainly proved to be far more effective than a conventional basement humidifier, which was the preferred solution of other contractors.

The totality and thoroughness of the remediation effort has kept the humidity of the house below 50%, which is critical for mold suppression.

David A. Rich, Ph.D.
Electrical Engineering Professor
Easton, Pennsylvania
