We have often been called upon to improve the air quality in medical buildings but up until now the options have been limited and results not always long term. Our target air change rate is 6/hr. 6 seems to be the general standard for medical facilities - a single unit is capable of meeting 6 air changes an hour in a room of up to 2500ft^3.

Kill Rates:
The data shows a single-pass kill rate of between 95-99% for many common pathogens (MRSA, influenza A, TB, parvovirus), even at unloaded, 450CFM airflow, for all air entering the unit. Inactivation rate increases significantly per-pass, to the point where nearly all the virus/bacterial pathogens reach an inactivation rate of 99.99% after just 2 passes.
Some of the hardier bacteria and molds are harder to kill

  • VRE average single-pass inactivation: 84.90%
  • C. diff: 18.30%
  • aspergillus: 3.61%
but for the most part, multiple passes reach acceptable numbers
  • VRE reaches 99.66% kill rate after 3 passes
  • C. diff reaches 70.25% after 6 passes
.
Molds and fungi are the hardiest (average kill rate for aspergillus is 19.80% after 6 passes), but the presence of the pre-filter and HEPA filter compensates, not just by trapping them, but by forcing long-term UVC exposure once trapped in the filters, preventing any mold/fungus issues within the system itself.
